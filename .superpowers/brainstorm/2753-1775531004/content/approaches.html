How should the site be structured?

Three approaches — each mirrors the knowtogo.pt aesthetic but organises content differently

A ⭐

Landmark-Driven Cards RECOMMENDED

Homepage showcases the 10 tour highlights as rich photo cards (cave paintings, ochre mine, flying-fox colony, etc.) — exactly like knowtogo.pt treats destinations. Each card links to a detailed stop page. Clean nav, scroll-reveal sections, a "Book Now" CTA that's always visible.

Pros

  • Directly mirrors knowtogo.pt card grid
  • Each highlight gets its own spotlight
  • Visually rich from the first scroll
  • Easy to grow (add more stops/content)

Cons

  • Needs good photos for each stop
B

Storytelling Scroll

The homepage is a single narrative journey — scrolling tells the full story of Country, Jessy's background, and the tour path in sequence. Full-bleed imagery, large text overlays, map timeline of the walk. Cards and grids appear on inner pages only.

Pros

  • Immersive and emotionally powerful
  • Strong cultural storytelling
  • Unique — stands out from competitors

Cons

  • More complex to build
  • Less "scannable" for quick info
  • Heavy on photos to pull off
C

Hybrid — Story Hero + Card Grid

Hero section tells the story with a cinematic full-width introduction (quote from Jessy, Welcome to Country, landscape image). Below the fold: card grid of tour highlights. Inner pages are content-focused. Best of both — emotional hook then practical detail.

Pros

  • Emotional + practical balance
  • Knowtogo.pt feel with Indigenous heart
  • Works even with fewer photos

Cons

  • Hero section needs strong copywriting
