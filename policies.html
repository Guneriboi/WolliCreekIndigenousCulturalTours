Bookings & Refunds Policy

Wolli Creek Valley Cultural Guided Tours — 2025

Bookings

All tour bookings are made directly with Jessy Haiyang via email or phone. A booking is confirmed once Jessy has acknowledged your enquiry and confirmed the tour date, group size, and meeting details in writing.

Payment is due at the time of booking confirmation. Jessy will provide bank transfer details upon confirmation. Tours are not held without payment.

Cancellations by the Client

  • More than 7 days before the tour: Full refund provided.
  • 3–7 days before the tour: 50% refund, or the option to reschedule to another available date at no cost.
  • Less than 3 days before the tour: No refund. Reschedule option available at Jessy's discretion.
  • No-show on the day: No refund.

Cancellations by Wolli Creek Cultural Tours

Jessy reserves the right to cancel or reschedule a tour in the event of severe weather, health or safety concerns, or other circumstances outside her control. In these cases, all clients will receive a full refund or be offered a reschedule at no cost.

Cancellations due to weather will be communicated by 7:00 AM on the day of the tour via the contact details provided at booking.

Group Bookings

For private group bookings of 8 or more participants, a 25% deposit is required at the time of booking. The balance is due 7 days before the tour. Cancellation terms above apply to the total booking amount.

Safe Work Plan

Workplace Health & Safety Policy and Procedures — Wolli Creek Valley Cultural Guided Tours

Purpose

This Safe Work Plan outlines the health and safety procedures for all guided tour operations conducted by Wolli Creek Valley Cultural Guided Tours. The safety of all participants and the guide is the highest priority on every tour.

Before the Tour

  • Weather forecast checked. Tours cancelled in severe weather (thunderstorms, extreme heat above 40°C, or total fire ban days).
  • First aid kit carried by guide on all tours.
  • Guide to carry mobile phone with full charge and emergency contacts saved.
  • Participant numbers confirmed — maximum 15 persons per tour.
  • All participants briefed on safety procedures at the start of the tour.

On-Tour Safety Briefing

At the start of every tour, the guide will:

  • Identify themselves and confirm the tour route and duration.
  • Ask participants to disclose any medical conditions, allergies, or mobility requirements.
  • Point out any known hazards on the route (uneven ground, creek crossings, slippery surfaces).
  • Instruct participants to stay together and not wander from the group.
  • Provide emergency contact details and the location of the nearest first aid kit.

Hazards and Controls

  • Uneven terrain: Participants advised to wear appropriate footwear. Slow pace on difficult sections.
  • Sun and heat: Participants advised to bring hat, sunscreen, and minimum 1L water. Tours not conducted in extreme heat.
  • Wildlife: Participants instructed not to approach, feed, or touch wildlife. Guide maintains safe distance from flying-fox colony.
  • Bush foods tasting: Only known safe species offered. Participants with food allergies asked to declare at the start of the tour.
  • Creek crossings: Guide assesses creek level before crossing. High water: crossing bypassed.
  • Medical emergency: Guide trained in First Aid. Call 000 for emergencies. Nearest hospital: St George Hospital, Kogarah.

Incident Reporting

Any incident, near-miss, or injury occurring during a tour must be documented by the guide within 24 hours. Serious incidents must be reported to Safe Work NSW as required by law.

Property & Land Acknowledgement

Wolli Creek Valley and Turrella Reserve — Property Information

Crown Lands NSW

Tours operate within the Wolli Creek Valley Regional Park and Turrella Reserve, which are managed by Crown Lands NSW and local councils. All tour operations comply with relevant Crown Lands regulations and any conditions attached to the use of these lands for guided tours.

Cultural Sites

Cave paintings and other cultural heritage sites visited on the tour are protected under the NSW Heritage Act 1977 and the Aboriginal Land Rights Act 1983. Participants must not touch, mark, or disturb any cultural heritage site. Photography is permitted from a respectful distance.

Leave No Trace

All participants are expected to respect the environment. No litter, no disturbance of native plants or wildlife, and no collection of natural materials (rocks, plants, soil) from the reserve.

Bush Regeneration

Five percent of all tour revenue is directed to bush regeneration activities in the Wolli Creek Valley, including native planting and weed removal. This is a direct commitment by Wolli Creek Valley Cultural Tours to the ongoing health of the Country on which tours operate.
